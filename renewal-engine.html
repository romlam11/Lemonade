Core Insight

Car insurance has hard renewal cycles — 6 or 12 months. A perfectly crafted offer sent 3 months after renewal gets ignored. The same offer sent 30 days before renewal, when the customer is evaluating options and their current carrier is sending renewal notices, converts at dramatically higher rates. Yet Lemonade currently has no mechanism to identify or capitalize on this window. The Renewal Intelligence Engine solves this by collecting one simple data point — when does your car insurance renew? — and building an activation system around it.

🎯

Objective

Increase the renters-to-car bundle conversion rate by capturing car insurance renewal dates and activating personalized cross-sell offers 30–45 days before renewal — when switching intent naturally peaks.

👥

Target Audience

Existing Lemonade renters-only customers who already have car insurance with a competitor, are in a state where Lemonade offers car insurance (~60% of the base), and whose renewal window is approaching within 45 days.

⚡

Why This Wins

Cross-sold customers cost effectively $0 to acquire, generate 3× the premium of single-policy holders, and retain at 91–95% vs. 67–85%. This feature doesn't create new demand — it captures existing demand at the moment it peaks.

🔑

Value Exchange

Customers share their renewal date because Lemonade offers something in return: a free price check, a reminder so they don't auto-renew without comparing, and estimated savings. The framing is "let us make sure you're not overpaying" — a service, not a sales pitch.

Risk Mitigation
Anticipated Risks & Mitigations
High Risk
Low Capture Rate

Customers may not share or know their renewal date. Mitigation: The "I'm not sure" path still captures the customer as an inferred target. Month-level granularity (not exact date) reduces friction. The savings hook provides clear value exchange.

Medium Risk
Push Notification Fatigue

Customers may perceive the notification as spam. Mitigation: V1 sends exactly one notification per cycle. "As promised" framing connects to the opt-in moment. Dismiss-twice suppression prevents re-targeting unhappy users.

Medium Risk
Price Competitiveness

Lemonade's car rates may not beat the customer's current carrier. Mitigation: Lead with estimated savings, not guaranteed. If Lemonade can't beat the rate, Maya acknowledges it honestly — building trust for the next cycle. The bundle discount often offsets small price differences.

Medium Risk
State Availability Gap

Car insurance only available in ~10 states (~60% of base). Mitigation: Only collect and activate for eligible customers. For ineligible states, store the data — it becomes instantly valuable when Lemonade expands to that state.
