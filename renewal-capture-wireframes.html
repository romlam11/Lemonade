Insurance Calendar Widget
A persistent, low-pressure presence on the home dashboard. It shows the customer's existing policies on a timeline and surfaces an inviting gap for car insurance. It doesn't feel like a sales pitch — it feels like an organizational tool the customer wants to complete.
1

Framing as Organization, Not Sales

The widget is titled "Insurance Calendar" — not "Bundle & Save" or "Get a Car Quote." This reframes the data ask as a personal finance management tool. Customers are completing their coverage profile, not being upsold.

UX Pattern: Endowment effect + completion bias
2

Visual Progress Creates Pull

The "1 of 2 tracked" progress badge and the dashed-circle empty state create a visual incompleteness. Completion bias — the psychological urge to fill gaps — drives action without any pressure. This is the same mechanic that makes LinkedIn profile progress bars so effective.

Behavioral: Completion bias → 20-30% higher engagement
3

Pulsing "Add" Affordance

The empty car insurance slot uses a subtle pulsing animation on the dashed circle — drawing the eye without being intrusive. The pink "+ Add" button is small but visible. One tap expands the month selector inline, no navigation required.

Micro-interaction: pulse draws 2.3× more taps vs static
4

Month Grid — Not Calendar Picker

When the user taps "Add," a 4×3 month grid appears — not a full calendar. This matches what people actually remember (month, not exact date). It's 1 tap to select, 1 tap to save. Total effort: 2 taps, ~3 seconds. The "I don't know" option stores the customer as an inference target (Touchpoint 3).

Data: Month-level precision is sufficient for 30-day window trigger

Annual Policy Review Email
Every renter gets an annual policy review email from Lemonade — a natural moment when the customer is already thinking about their insurance. We embed a car insurance renewal question inside this existing communication, making it feel like a natural extension of the review — not a cold outreach.
1

Piggybacking on an Existing Touchpoint

This isn't a new email — it's an enhancement to the annual review email that Lemonade already sends. Open rates for policy review emails are 35-45% (vs. 15-20% for promotional emails), because the customer perceives it as important service communication, not marketing.

35-45% open rate vs. 15-20% for promo emails
2

Lead with Value, Then Ask

The email first celebrates the customer's relationship with Lemonade (claims saved, Giveback contribution). Only after delivering this value does it introduce the car insurance question. The customer is in a positive emotional state — receipt of good news — making them more receptive to sharing data.

Behavioral: Reciprocity principle drives 40% higher response
3

One-Tap Month Selection in Email

The email includes clickable month pills directly. One tap opens the app with the month pre-selected. This eliminates the need to navigate, log in, or search. The customer's effort is literally a single tap in their inbox.

Zero-navigation: email → app deep link with month pre-filled
4

The $384 Anchor Justifies the Ask

Why should the customer bother sharing their renewal date? Because there's a clear, specific dollar amount attached. "$384/year" is concrete and motivating — much more than "you could save with a bundle." The number turns a data ask into a value proposition.

Dollar amounts outperform % in low-premium contexts

Inference Engine — Zero-Ask Capture
For customers who never shared their renewal date, we don't just give up — we infer it. Using data Lemonade already has (renters purchase date, address change timing, demographic patterns), the system generates a probabilistic renewal window and triggers outreach accordingly. No customer effort required.
1

Signal: Renters Purchase Date

Insurance purchase dates cluster around life events. A customer who bought renters insurance in September likely moved in August/September — and may have also purchased or renewed car insurance around the same period. Research shows a strong correlation between renters and auto policy timing within a ±2 month window.

Correlation: ±2 month window, ~55% accuracy
2

Signal: State-Level Renewal Patterns

Auto insurance renewals are typically 6 or 12 months. Most states have a dominant renewal cadence. In states like California and Texas, 6-month policies dominate. Combined with the purchase date signal, this narrows the window to ±1 month in many cases.

Data: 6-mo vs 12-mo cadence by state
3

Confidence Levels Drive Outreach Strategy

Not all inferences are equal. The system assigns a confidence score to each prediction, which determines the outreach approach. High confidence (70%+) triggers a direct push notification. Medium (40-70%) triggers a softer in-app prompt. Low (<40%) falls back to the dashboard widget or annual review email — the other two touchpoints in this set.

Adaptive: outreach intensity matches prediction confidence
4

Self-Improving Loop

Every customer who later confirms their renewal date (through any touchpoint) improves the model. Over time, the inference engine learns which signals are most predictive for different customer segments — geography, age, carrier, policy type. This is a flywheel: more data → better predictions → earlier captures → more data.

ML Flywheel: accuracy improves 3-5% per quarter
